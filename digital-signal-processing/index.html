The Fundamental Transforms

The four equations that unify all of digital signal processing

Nyquist–Shannon Sampling Theorem
$$x(t) \text{ is recoverable from samples iff } f_s > 2f_{\max}$$

A bandlimited signal can be perfectly reconstructed from its samples if the sampling rate exceeds twice the highest frequency component.

Convolution Theorem
$$Y(e^{j\omega}) = X(e^{j\omega}) \cdot H(e^{j\omega})$$

Convolution in time domain equals multiplication in frequency domain — the key insight enabling efficient filter analysis.

Discrete Fourier Transform
$$X[k] = \sum_{n=0}^{N-1} x[n]\, W_N^{kn}, \quad W_N = e^{-j2\pi/N}$$

Maps a finite sequence to its frequency-domain representation — the computationally realizable form of spectral analysis.

Z-Transform
$$X(z) = \sum_{n=-\infty}^{\infty} x[n]\, z^{-n}$$

Generalizes the DTFT to the entire complex plane, enabling algebraic system analysis via poles and zeros.
